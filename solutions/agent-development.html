Common Questions

Custom AI agent FAQ

What is a custom AI agent?

A custom AI agent is an AI system purpose-built for one specific role's workflow — with access to that role's tools, data, and context — rather than a generic chatbot. It takes actions inside your systems, follows your business rules, and is designed around how the person it partners with actually works.

How is a custom AI agent different from ChatGPT or an off-the-shelf tool?

Off-the-shelf tools answer general questions with general context. A custom AI agent knows your systems, your processes, and the specific role it partners with. It can access your CRM, your reporting tools, and your documentation — and it follows your rules for what good output looks like in your organization. Our guide on custom AI agents vs. off-shelf tools walks through when each approach is right.

How long does it take to build a custom AI agent?

A focused first agent typically takes 6 to 12 weeks from scoping to production for a team-scale use case. Timeline depends primarily on integration complexity and evaluation depth, not on model selection. We recommend starting small — one role, one agent — to compress the first timeline. See our 90-day AI adoption timeline for the week-by-week view.

What does a custom AI agent cost?

Expect $40,000 to $250,000 for a team-scale first agent, depending on integrations, evaluation depth, and security review requirements. Enterprise-wide agents with deep system integration land higher. The first agent is the most expensive; subsequent agents in the same organization typically cost 30 to 50 percent less. For the full picture, see hidden costs beyond the technology budget.

What integrations do custom AI agents support?

We integrate with the systems your role already uses: CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot), ERP (SAP, Workday, NetSuite), collaboration (Slack, Teams, Google Workspace, Microsoft 365), support (Zendesk, Intercom), and custom databases or APIs. Enterprise identity via SSO, SAML, and SCIM is standard. If your role uses it, the agent can usually reach it.

How do you handle enterprise security and compliance?

Custom agents are built with your security team from day one. Typical controls include SSO and role-based access, data isolation per deployment, full audit logging, no training on your data, and support for SOC 2 and vendor risk reviews. Deployment happens in your environment or a dedicated tenant, not a shared one.
